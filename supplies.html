Supplies

This designer exports a letter-size PDF with nine labels per sheet. After you print, cut, and apply them to blank NFC cards, you are ready to write tags with the Zaparoo app. The items below are what most people use — search links point to Amazon for convenience (not affiliate links).

Essentials

Blank NTAG215 NFC cards

Zaparoo works best with NTAG215 chips — they have enough memory for launch commands. Look for standard credit-card size blanks (about 54 × 85 mm). Avoid MIFARE Classic tags; they are not recommended for new Zaparoo setups. Search on Amazon

Matte vinyl sticker paper (US Letter)

This designer lays out labels on 8.5 × 11″ (Letter) sheets. Matte vinyl is a popular choice in the Zaparoo community: it prints cleanly on most inkjet printers, handles well, and takes lamination if you want extra durability. Search on Amazon

Cutting & finishing

Corner rounder (R3 / 3 mm)

NFC cards have rounded corners. A 3 mm (R3) corner rounder is the closest match and gives labels a finished look. Clean adhesive buildup from the punch every few cuts so it does not jam. Search on Amazon

Cutting mat, metal ruler & craft knife

The PDF includes cut marks, but many people still trim labels with an X-Acto-style knife and a straight edge. A self-healing mat protects your desk and keeps cuts accurate. Search on Amazon

3D-printed sticker applicator

TapTo Sticker Applicator

Applying full-size labels by hand can be fiddly — bubbles and crooked edges add up fast. This free 3D-printable jig by EntirelyTom helps hold an NFC card and sticker in alignment while you apply the label edge-first, then push the card through to finish the rest.

The model ships as a single 3MF file with separate parts. Load them into your slicer as individual objects so you can rotate and arrange them for your bed. After printing, use CA glue to attach the rounded applicator to the frame (and optionally the first crossbar). It takes a little practice, but it beats freestyling on the table.

  1. Slide the NFC card into the jig.
  2. Apply the sticker to the first crossbar, leaving a little overhang.
  3. Align the card below and press the sticker onto the top edge of the card.
  4. Push the card through the jig to apply the rest of the label.
